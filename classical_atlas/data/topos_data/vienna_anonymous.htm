Vienna Anonymous, Theaters and Schools of Athens

Vienna Anonymous, The Theaters and Schools of Athens, roughly translated by Brady Kiesling from the text of Vienna Cod. Theolog. Gr. 252, fol. 39/32 printed by Ludwig Ross in Archäologische Aufsätze (Band 1): Griechische Gräber. Ausgrabungsberichte aus Athen. Zur Kunstgeschichte und Topographie von Athen und Attika — Leipzig, 1855, p 251-4. Greek at digi.ub.uni-heidelberg.de/diglit/ross1855bd1 This anonymous, undated short account of Athenian antiquities is a reminder of how vague an idea of history survived in medieval Athens during the Frankish period. The text has significant gaps, and is sometimes unintelligible. This text has 20 tagged references to 18 ancient places.

§ 1  The Theaters and Schools [didaskaleia] of Athens
First is the Academy in one of the royal estates (? εν χωριω των Βασιλικών); second the Eleatic in Ampelokepoi; third, the school of Plato in the walled garden (paradeision); fourth, that of Polyzelos in Mt. Hymettos; fifth, that of Diodoros near it.

Event Date: 500 GR

§ 2  Inside the city is the school of Socrates, in which are depicted in a circle the men and the winds. West of this are the palaces of Themistocles. And near them are the splendid houses of the polemarch. The statues of Zeus stand very near them. Opposite them is an altar at which Pankratiasts and Olympians earn the right of burial. The orators frequent this place to read their epitaphic speeches.

Event Date: 500 GR

§ 3  North of this was the first agora of the city, where the Apostle Philip sank the secretary. Here were the splendid houses of the Pandionid tribe. In the southern part was the school of the Cynic philosophers. And near this that of the Tragedians. Outside the Acropolis, a little to the west, the Thalam[...9-10 letters] used to live. And near this was the school of Sophocles. And south of this was the Areopagos. Here is where Halirrothios the son of Poseidon was killed by Ares.

Event Date: 500 GR

§ 4  East of this were the palaces of Kleonides (?) and Miltiades, and near them further on was the [theater] school called Aristotle's. Above it were two columns, and on the eastern one was the statue of Athena, while on the west was that of Poseidon. Between them they say was once the Gorgon head, inside an iron enclosure [κουβουκλειον]. There is also a marble sundial.

Event Date: 500 GR

§ 5  Opposite them to the south was the school called Aristophanes'. And further eastward stands the Lantern of Demosthenes. Near this was once the house of Thucydides, and of Solon, and the second agora. And the house of Alcmaeon, and a very large bath. And south of it the great Agora of the city. And an extremely admirable temple by the south gate, on the gateposts of which are depicted nineteen men... the one he ousted. There was the royal bath, in which they wanted to frighten Basil the Great with thunderclaps. There was the house of the Mnestarch (mystarch?)

Event Date: 500 GR

§ 6  To the east of this stands a huge and beautiful archway. It has the names of Hadrian and Theseus. Inside the courtyard is located a very large ...[gap of several words]. In it was the royal house, supported from underneath by many columns, which was finely worked by the twelve kings who constructed the top.

Event Date: 500 GR

§ 7  South of these is a beautiful royal house. The Dux used to go down to it occasionally for celebrations. There is the Enneakrounos (nine-spouted] Kallirroe spring, where after bathing he used to go up to the precinct called that of Hera and pray there. But now it has been turned by the faithful into the temple of the super-holy Mother of God (υπεραγίας θεοτόκου)

Event Date: 500 GR

§ 8  East of these is the theater of Athens, enclosed in a circle, as if a space of a mile, with two entrances ... northern entrance with a wealth of ..., the other ... extends ...southern. It was ornamented with one hundred bands in a circle, which were made of white marble, in which the people sat to watch the competition and the wrestling.

Event Date: 500 GR

§ 9  Entering the eastern gate from here we find another Agora and two aqueducts, which Julius Caesar built for the Athenians as a gift, and brought water to them from afar. There is another aqueduct at the north gate, which Theseus crafted. And this happened after the tyranny of the city of Athens, as Abaris and Herodotus say, it was crafted by twelve kings. Cecrops of the double nature brightened it up greatly, ornamenting it in variegated glory. For he raised the walls high, and paved the ground with various marbles, and gilding the temene inside and out he named this city Athens.

Event Date: 500 GR

§ 10  When we entered the Acropolis we found a small school of the musicians, which Pythagoras the Samian put together. Opposite it is a great palace, and underneath it stands a great wealth of tiles and marble, with the roof and walls. Toward the north cliff was a whole chancelry (καγγελαρία) made of marble and white columns. To the south of it [or in back of it] was the stoa in most beautiful paintwork, gilded inside and outside all around and decorated with precious stones. The philosophers who apprenticed here were called Stoics on account of this. Opposite this flourished the school of the Epicureans.

Event Date: 500 GR

§ 11  Regarding the temple of the Mother of God, which Apollos and Eulogios built in the name of the Unknown God, the following: It is a very long (δρομικώτατος) and spacious temple, much extended in length. And its walls are made of white marble. It stands on a rectangular plan, without clay and lime. Each wall is erected with iron and lead. Outside the wall a wealth of huge columns surrounds the temple in a circuit. Between the two columns there is obliqueness. Toward the beautiful gate [central door of the iconostasis] and the holy bema, which are to the west [or SW], and thras[...half-line gap] the position of the columns is extended, projecting a fair amount in height. The capitals of the columns are joined together with iron carvings in the shape of a palm tree. They are reused. Above these are beams made of white marble bonded to the walls, having plaques joined from above like a roof, which appears convex. A very beautiful wall is also supported by the columns.

Event Date: 500 GR
